The Missed Call Problem

Why Calls Get Missed

Your front desk staff aren't slacking—they're overwhelmed. During peak hours, they're simultaneously:

  • Checking in arriving patients
  • Processing payments and insurance
  • Answering patient questions in-office
  • Coordinating with clinical staff
  • Managing the schedule

The phone becomes priority #5 or #6. And after hours? 100% of calls go to voicemail.

When Calls Get Missed

Data from analyzing 50+ dental practices shows clear patterns:

  • 11am-1pm: Lunch hour + morning rush overlap
  • 4pm-6pm: End of day chaos
  • After 6pm: Zero coverage for 30% of total calls
  • Weekends: Zero coverage for 15% of total calls

Your Options for Phone Coverage

Option 1: Hire Additional Staff

Cost: $35,000-50,000/year

The traditional solution. Hire another front desk person dedicated to phones.

Pros: Human interaction, can handle complex situations

Cons: Expensive, limited hours, sick days, turnover, training costs

Option 2: Traditional Answering Service

Cost: $200-500/month + per-call fees

Outsourced call centers with live operators who take messages and forward to your team.

Pros: 24/7 coverage, no hiring

Cons: Can't book appointments, generic scripts, per-call fees add up, long hold times

Option 3: AI Receptionist

Cost: $299-999/month flat rate

AI-powered systems that answer calls, have natural conversations, and book appointments directly into your calendar.

Pros: 24/7, no hold times, books appointments, knows your practice, flat-rate pricing

Cons: Can't handle every edge case (transfers to humans when needed)
